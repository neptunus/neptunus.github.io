Hello~!
If you're here, you might be looking for some information about me; so bear with me as I try to communicate a little something about who I am. Any time I try this, it feels reductive. I guess that's the point, though.

My name is Neptúnus, and I suppose you could describe me as a computers person. I make websites and apps, am comfortable in a variety of operating systems, manage quite well in both simple text-editors and full-blown IDEs, enjoy games and read a lot of text online. Since my first encounter with a Mac in my childhood, I've been enthralled by glowing monitors and the stuff we put on them.

Other interests include science fiction and other literarure, fundamental human rights, hiking, cooking, bodyweight fitness, and the arts; admiring and taking part in them. I have a particularly soft spot for pixel-art, and have dabbled in it a little myself.
I aspire to improve my writing.

I went to school for design and art at Fjölbrautaskólinn í Breiðholti. Later on, I studied computer science at the Reykjavík Technical College. Since then, I've learned a lot on my own through work and side-projects. Mostly, I've come to admire simplicity in all things.

Nowadays, I'm employed as a developer at Gangverk; modeling content, writing components, updating documentation and learning the ins and outs of the business we're working for. Before I started doing all that, I did a bit of freelance work. Using Wordpress to make customized e-commerce environments, including a site that features VOD & paywalled courses, and an exhibition space that works in conjunction with hundreds of artists at a time. I made a web-app that can generate personalized pixel-art avatars, rendering the customized art in Daði Freyr's distinctive style and exporting to be printed on personal sweatshirts or used on social media as a profile picture. I've worked on native iOS apps, set up backend systems in Python and brought visual designs to life for everything from entire websites to marketing emails.

As I continue writing, I get the urge to set up some kind of system to manage posts of different kinds. I want to maintain an RSS feed, post artwork, blog—or even microblog…
This happens to a lot of us, I think.
